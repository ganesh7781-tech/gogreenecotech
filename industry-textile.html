Solving the Textile Industry's Waste Challenge

In the textile sector, waste problems often stem from poor process control, inadequate housekeeping, and obsolete technology. Chemical sludges from dyeing and finishing processes pose a significant environmental risk if not handled correctly.

Common Waste Categories in Textiles

Chemical Sludges

Highly concentrated residue from wastewater treatment plants (ETP) containing dyes and chemicals.

Cotton Flub

Fibrous waste generated during spinning and weaving that requires efficient collection and storage.

Dye Residues

Spent dye liquors and pigment wastes that must be processed to prevent water pollution.

Our Processing Technology

We utilize advanced filtration and homogenization techniques to reduce the volume of textile sludge, making it safer for final disposal or co-processing. Our team ensures that every stage from collection to the final certificate follows CPCB guidelines.
